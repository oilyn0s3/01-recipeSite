Gelatin. Gelatin is used to thicken and turn liquid foods into a solid gummy gel. Unlike the plant-based agar-agar, Gelatin is derived from collagen found in the skin and bones of animals, most notably from pigs, as MCME points out.A gelatin-based foam, like the Rainbow Foam below, is created when partially set gelatin is agitated, usually with a whisk, to incorporate air and bubbles into the gelatin mixture which is then trapped once the gelatin cools, much like how water is trapped in the standard formation of Jello. The trapped air results in a light and airy texture.
